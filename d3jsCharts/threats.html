Common Cyber Security Threats

Understanding the threats you face is the first step to protecting your organization. Here are some of the most common types of cyber attacks:

Phishing & Social Engineering

Phishing attacks are fraudulent attempts—via email, SMS, phone, or social media—to trick people into revealing sensitive information or installing malware. Attackers impersonate trusted brands or colleagues and use urgent language to push quick action.

Defense: Train staff to verify unusual requests through a separate channel, inspect sender details and links, and report suspicious messages. Use email filtering, safe-link scanning, and block known malicious domains.

Ransomware

Ransomware is malicious software that encrypts company data and demands payment for decryption. It can halt operations, cause data loss, and trigger reputational and regulatory consequences.

Defense: Keep offline/immutable backups, patch systems promptly, restrict admin rights, and use endpoint protection with behavior-based detection. Drill an incident response plan and test restore procedures regularly.

Business Email Compromise (BEC)

BEC scams impersonate executives, suppliers, or partners to trick staff into approving payments or sharing sensitive data. Messages often look legitimate and may contain no malware.

Defense: Require multi-person approvals for payments and changes to bank details, verify by phone using known numbers, and implement SPF, DKIM, and DMARC to reduce spoofing.

Weak or Stolen Passwords

Weak, default, or reused passwords allow attackers to guess access or reuse leaked credentials from other breaches (credential stuffing) to enter business systems.

Defense: Enforce strong, unique passwords with a password manager and require multi-factor authentication (MFA) on all critical systems and accounts.

Malware & Viruses

Malware such as trojans, spyware, and worms can steal data, monitor activity, or disrupt operations. It often arrives via malicious attachments, drive-by downloads, or infected USB devices.

Defense: Deploy reputable endpoint protection, block unauthorized apps, disable autorun for removable media, and limit software installation to approved sources.

Insider Threats

Insiders—employees, contractors, or partners—may misuse access intentionally (theft, sabotage) or accidentally (mistakes that expose data), causing significant harm.

Defense: Apply least-privilege access, monitor activity on sensitive systems, separate duties for high-risk tasks, and ensure thorough onboarding/offboarding processes.

Supply Chain Attacks

Attackers compromise trusted vendors, software updates, or managed service providers to gain indirect access to your systems and data.

Defense: Assess vendors’ security, include security clauses in contracts, monitor third-party access, and keep supplier-provided software promptly updated.

Cloud & SaaS Misconfigurations

Misconfigured cloud storage or lax SaaS permissions can expose sensitive information or enable unauthorized access to business data and integrations.

Defense: Use strong access controls and MFA, regularly audit configurations (CIS benchmarks), enable encryption at rest/in transit, and log/monitor admin actions.

Denial-of-Service (DoS/DDoS)

DoS/DDoS attacks overwhelm websites or online services with traffic, making them unreachable and potentially masking other intrusions.

Defense: Use hosting/CDN providers with DDoS protection, deploy a Web Application Firewall (WAF), and prepare a service continuity and communication plan.

Unpatched & Outdated Software

Known vulnerabilities in unpatched operating systems, applications, or network devices provide easy entry points for attackers.

Defense: Maintain an asset and patch inventory, enable automatic updates where safe, prioritize critical patches, and regularly scan for vulnerabilities.

Mobile & Remote Work Risks

Personal or poorly secured devices and home networks broaden the attack surface. Lost or stolen devices may expose business data.

Defense: Enforce device encryption, screen locks, and remote wipe. Require VPN for remote access, manage devices (MDM/EMM), and install endpoint protection.

Data Breaches

Unauthorized access to customer, employee, or financial data can lead to financial loss, legal penalties, and reputational damage.

Defense: Classify and encrypt sensitive data, restrict access on a need-to-know basis, monitor for anomalies, and maintain a tested incident response and notification plan.
