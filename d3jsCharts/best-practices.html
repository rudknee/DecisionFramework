Cyber Security Best Practices

Following best practices is crucial for maintaining a strong and resilient cyber security posture. Here are some fundamental practices every organization should adopt:

1. Implement a Strong Password Policy

Use strong, unique passwords for all accounts and systems. A strong password should be long (at least 12 characters) and include a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using easily guessable information. Enforce password changes every 90 days.

2. Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA adds a second layer of security beyond just a password. Even if an attacker steals a password, they won't be able to access the account without the second factor (e.g., a code from a smartphone app). Enable MFA wherever it is available, especially for critical systems like email and financial applications.

3. Keep Software and Systems Updated

Cybercriminals often exploit known vulnerabilities in software to gain access to systems. Regularly update your operating systems, applications (e.g., web browsers, office suites), and security software. Enable automatic updates whenever possible to ensure you're protected against the latest threats.

4. Regular Data Backups

Regularly back up all critical business data. Your backup strategy should follow the 3-2-1 rule: have at least three copies of your data, on two different media types, with one copy stored off-site. Periodically test your backups to ensure you can restore the data successfully in an emergency.

5. Train Your Employees

Your employees are your first line of defense. Conduct regular security awareness training to educate them about common threats like phishing, social engineering, and safe internet habits. Create a culture where security is everyone's responsibility.

6. Secure Your Wi-Fi Networks

Change the default administrator password on your router. Use a strong, unique password for your Wi-Fi network and use WPA2 or WPA3 encryption. Consider creating a separate guest network for visitors to keep your main business network more secure.

For additional guidance, please visit the Business Guide on Cybersecurity for SMEs by the NCSC
